Notice: Temporary Closure of Rare Books Section

Published: April 22, 2025

Due to a recent containment issue, the Rare Books section will be temporarily closed to the public effective immediately. During a routine archival inspection, staff identified unusual signs of degradation among several historically significant volumes. Investigation has found that the cause was from rodents, breaking in.

While the affected materials have been isolated and there is no immediate threat to visitor safety, we are proceeding with deep preservation cleaning and environmental recalibration of the archives.

Please note:

  • All access to the Rare Books catalog has been suspended until further notice.
  • Researchers with scheduled appointments will be contacted individually.
  • Restoration is expected to take 1–2 weeks, pending results from external bioassay testing.

We appreciate your understanding and patience as we work to protect these irreplaceable collections.

Cognitive Enhancement & Human Integration Trial

Published: April 15, 2025

Collins Library is proud to support a groundbreaking research initiative in partnership with the Neuropath Research Institute. This joint venture, titled “Cognitive Enhancement & Human Integration Trial (CEHIT)”, aims to break the bounderies of human evolution, in thier cognitive and physicality.

Over the course of 2 months, registered and approved participants will engage in testing by researchers, including our head, Layan Tremblay. These sessions are facilitated through select archival materials.

Objectives:

  • Enhance human information processing capacity.
  • Improve strength, adaptation and constitution.
  • Study the Human cognitive and test genetic engineering.

Participants are monitored closely for changes in comprehension, body, and behavioral expression. Community members are invited to learn more during our public information session on May 4, 2025 at 6:30 PM, hosted in the Main Auditorium.
